Review: The Verge

by Robert Tayco | January 29, 2013
Link: The Verge

The Verge is a blog about technology, science, culture, and entertainment. They review tech products, report on breaking news, and write long-form feature stories on a variety of topics relevant to their audience. The site, built on Vox Media's multi-media Chorus platform, is densely packed with data and imagery linking to dozens of articles, videos, and reviews throughout their site. The overall look is beautifully designed, but the amount of imagery and media can bog the site down, making it very slow to load at times and especially on slower connections.

Review: Polygon

by Robert Tayco | January 29, 2013
Link: Polygon

Polygon is a blog about gaming, the people who make games, and the people who play the games. Like The Verge, Polygon is built on the Chorus platform by Vox Media. The site is built on an updated, fully responsive version of the platform and gracefully resizes to the browser window all the way down to mobile resolutions. Both images and text as well as layout adapt to the resizing of the browser window, but as with The Verge, Polygon can suffer from slow loading and lagging responsiveness. The Vox Media platform is one of the most feature-heavy and cutting edge platforms for rich interaction and is willing to sacrifice some performance now in the name of delivering content in ways that few other sites can offer.
